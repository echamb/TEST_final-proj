ingredients:
  • 1 c unsalted butter, melted and cooled to room temperature
  • 1 1/4 c packed light brown sugar
  • 3/4 c granulated sugar
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, spooned and leveled
  • 1 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 c semisweet chocolate chips
  • flaky sea salt
instructions:
  • whisk the flour, salt, baking soda and cinnamon in a medium bowl.
  • whisk both sugars in a large bowl, breaking up any large chunks.
  • add the melted butter to sugar and whisk vigorously for 1 minute.
  • whisk in 1 egg with sugar-butter mixture, stirring until mixed.
  • whisk in the second egg and vanilla.
  • add dry ingredients to wet and stir to fully combine.
  • stir in the chocolate chips.
    • bake after the dough rests (recommended): 
  • scoop the dough into 2-ounce portions with a cookie scoop and place on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  • wrap sheet with plastic wrap and refrigerate at least 4 hours.
  • preheat the oven to 375ºF.
  • sprinkle cookies with flaky sea salt.
  • bake the cookies 3 inches apart on a parchment-lined baking sheet for 10-14 minutes, until the cookie turns golden brown.
  • let cool on the sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
